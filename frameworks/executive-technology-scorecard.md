# Framework: Executive Technology Scorecard

## Purpose

Provide a balanced executive scorecard for technology leadership, connecting reliability, delivery, cost, security, people and business value.

---

## Scorecard Dimensions

| Dimension | Example Indicators |
|---|---|
| Reliability | Availability, SLO compliance, MTTR, incident recurrence |
| Delivery | Deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate, backlog flow |
| Customer Experience | NPS, journey success rate, error rate, digital abandonment |
| Cost and Efficiency | Cloud spend, savings, budget variance, unit cost, waste ratio |
| Security and Risk | Vulnerability backlog, exposure risk, security gate compliance |
| Operations | Incident volume, change success, problem management, capacity risk |
| Engineering Quality | Test automation, technical debt, quality gates, defect escape rate |
| People and Leadership | Team engagement, attrition, capability development, succession risk |
| Business Alignment | OKR contribution, critical initiatives, value delivered |

---

## Executive Review Structure

1. Business context.
2. Technology health overview.
3. Critical risks and decisions required.
4. Progress against key initiatives.
5. Reliability and customer experience.
6. Cost, efficiency and investment trade-offs.
7. People and capability development.
8. Next 30/60/90-day actions.

---

## Decision Principles

- Indicators should drive action.
- Metrics must connect to business impact.
- Risk must be explicit and prioritized.
- Cost optimization must preserve resilience and security.
- Technical debt must be translated into business consequence.
- Team health is a leading indicator of delivery sustainability.
